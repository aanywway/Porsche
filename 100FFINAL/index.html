PORSCHE BRAND HISTORY

Porsche, one of the most iconic and prestigious automotive brands in the world, has a rich history dating back to the early 20th century. The company was founded in 1931 by Ferdinand Porsche, an Austrian automotive engineer, and started as a vehicle development consultancy and engineering firm. Porsche's early years were marked by collaborations with various car manufacturers, including Volkswagen and Auto Union, where Ferdinand Porsche played a key role in designing the legendary Volkswagen Beetle and Auto Union Grand Prix race cars. In 1939, Porsche developed the first prototype for what would become the Volkswagen Beetle, known as the "People's Car." This project laid the foundation for Porsche's close association with the Volkswagen Group, which would continue to this day. During World War II, Porsche was heavily involved in designing military vehicles for the German Army, including the famous Tiger tank.

After the war, Ferdinand Porsche's son, Ferry Porsche, established the Porsche car company in 1948, with the launch of the Porsche 356. This lightweight and nimble sports car became the first production vehicle to bear the Porsche name and set the stage for the brand's future success in the automotive industry.

The true breakthrough for Porsche came in 1963 with the introduction of the Porsche 911, a rear-engined sports car that would become an automotive icon. The 911's timeless design, exceptional performance, and driving dynamics solidified Porsche's reputation as a leading sports car manufacturer. Over the years, the 911 has undergone numerous iterations and upgrades, maintaining its position as one of the most coveted sports cars in the world.

Porsche's motorsport pedigree has been a driving force behind the brand's success and reputation for engineering excellence. The company has a long history of success in racing, with victories in prestigious events such as the 24 Hours of Le Mans, the Formula One World Championship, and the World Endurance Championship. The legendary Porsche 917, 956, and 962 race cars have become synonymous with endurance racing dominance, showcasing Porsche's commitment to performance and innovation on the track.

In addition to its motorsport achievements, Porsche has continued to expand its product lineup over the years. The brand introduced its first SUV, the Cayenne, in 2002, which proved to be a commercial success and opened up new market segments for Porsche. The Panamera luxury sedan followed in 2009, further diversifying Porsche's portfolio and appealing to a broader range of consumers.

In recent years, Porsche has made significant investments in electric and hybrid technology, with the introduction of the hybrid versions of its sports cars and SUVs, such as the 918 Spyder and the Cayenne E-Hybrid. The all-electric Porsche Taycan, launched in 2019, marked a new era for the brand, showcasing Porsche's commitment to sustainable mobility without compromising performance. Today, Porsche remains a symbol of luxury, performance, and innovation in the automotive industry. The brand's commitment to quality, engineering excellence, and driving pleasure has earned it a loyal following of enthusiasts and customers worldwide. With a rich history rooted in motorsport success and iconic sports cars, Porsche continues to push the boundaries of automotive technology and set the standard for performance and design in the industry.

The engines in the 911 models are based on the biturbo concept. Among the outstanding qualities of these units are their ability to develop high revs, exceptionally responsive response and impressive maximum torque, which is already achieved at low revs. Located at the rear of the car, the opposition engine is short and low by virtue of its design, which ensures a low center of gravity. The engine mounts, located closer to the middle of the vehicle, improve stability and help reduce vibrations.

The engines have two turbochargers as well as a charge air cooling system. This is an important part of the exhaust gas-driven turbocharging system and contributes significantly to simultaneously increasing power and reducing emissions.

The 8-speed Porsche Doppelkupplung (PDK) gearbox provides very fast gear changes literally in milliseconds, and without interrupting traction. The PDK is characterized by very sporty gear settings from 1st to 6th and economical upshifts (7th and 8th).

The multifunction sports steering wheel allows you to conveniently use the audio, telephone and navigation functions. The gearshift paddles are characterized by short strokes and ensure quick and precise shifts. On the GT sports steering wheel, additional thumb rests and a "zero" mark at 12 o'clock are hallmarks of the 911's sporty genes.

The 911 sports seats are highly comfortable and provide reliable support in high-speed cornering. In the Plus sports seats, excellent support is guaranteed by stiff side bolsters on the cushion and backrest. Another advantage: both seats are electrically adjustable with a memory package, providing greater comfort for both driver and front passenger.

For a sports car, the rear seats offer quite a high level of comfort. Thanks to the folding backrests, the space behind the seats with a volume of up to 264 liters can also be used for transporting luggage.
